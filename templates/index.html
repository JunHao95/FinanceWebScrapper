📖 How to Read These Results

Regime Detection (Hidden Markov Model)

  • What it is: A 2-state HMM fitted to daily log-returns. State 0 = calm/bull; State 1 = stressed/bear.
  • P(Stressed) chart: Filtered probability of being in the stressed regime. Values near 1 = model believes high-volatility / downward-trending conditions.
  • Price + shading chart: Price history with red bands wherever State 1 probability exceeds 0.5.
  • RISK_ON / RISK_OFF: Current regime label. RISK_OFF = latest observation most likely stressed. Descriptive signal, not a trade recommendation.

References: Hidden Markov Models  |  Hamilton Filter  |  Viterbi Algorithm

Portfolio MDP (Markov Decision Process)

  • What it is: 12-state MDP where each state = (SPY momentum, IEF momentum, volatility bucket). Actions = 5 SPY/IEF weight splits (100/0 → 0/100). Policy found via Policy Iteration on training data.
  • V* bar chart: Optimal state-value V*(s) — expected discounted cumulative return starting from state s under the optimal policy. Taller bar = more valuable regime. Zero bars = state never seen in training; policy there is untested.
  • Optimal Policy Table: Maps each (vol, momentum) state to the recommended SPY/IEF allocation. "0 months" in Test Visits = that state never occurred in the test window — treat those rows with caution.
  • Backtest chart: Cumulative return of MDP policy vs. static 50/50 benchmark over the held-out test period. Gap shows whether regime-switching adds value over passive allocation.
  • Sharpe vs CAGR trade-off: Higher CAGR with lower Sharpe than benchmark means the MDP earned more but took on more risk to do it.

References: Markov Decision Process  |  Policy Iteration  |  Bellman Equation  |  Sharpe Ratio

⚙️ Heston Model Calibration

Two-stage calibration (brute-force grid + Nelder-Mead) of Heston parameters {κ, θ, σᵥ, ρ, ν₀} against real market options. Reports MSE fit quality.

⏰ Note: Calibration may take 30–120 seconds depending on options liquidity. Use liquid tickers (SPY, QQQ, AAPL) for best results. Market hours recommended.

🦘 Merton Jump-Diffusion Calibration

Two-stage calibration (brute-force grid + Nelder-Mead) of Merton parameters {σ, λ, μⱼ, δⱼ} against real market options. Captures fat tails and jump risk.

⏰ Note: Calibration may take 30–90 seconds depending on options liquidity. Use liquid tickers (SPY, QQQ, AAPL) for best results.

📐 CIR Interest Rate Model

Cox-Ingersoll-Ross (1985) stochastic rate model: dr = κ(θ−r)dt + σ√r dW. Computes zero-coupon bond prices B(0,T) and implied yield curve.

Vasicek allows negative rates (no Feller condition). CIR guarantees non-negative rates.
