### Critical Gaps Found

#### BREAK-01 (HIGH) — Peer section silently empty on re-scrape

**Affected requirements:** PEER-01, PEER-02, PEER-03, PEER-04, PEER-05

`peerComparison.js:201` guards double-render: `if (_sessionCache[ticker]) return`. `PeerComparison.clearSession()` is never called anywhere in the application — not in `stockScraper.js`, `main.js`, or `displayManager.js`. The other three modules expose `clearSession()` as no-ops; only PeerComparison's has real work to do.

**Effect:** When a user scrapes tickers, sees results, then runs analysis again (same tickers or overlapping set), `renderIntoGroup` returns immediately at line 201 for any previously-seen ticker. No spinner appears, no fetch fires, the peer comparison section is entirely absent from the rebuilt card. The bug is silent — no error message, no console warning.

**Fix (1 line):** In `static/js/stockScraper.js`, before `tickerResultsDiv.innerHTML = ''` (line 188), add:
```javascript
if (typeof PeerComparison !== 'undefined') PeerComparison.clearSession();
```

#### BREAK-02 (LOW) — Stale DCF premium badge persists after Recalculate

**Affected requirements:** DCF-02, DCF-04

`buildHTML` renders an initial premium badge inline (`premiumHTML`) at line 202 and a hidden `<div id="dcf-premium-{ticker}">` at line 203. `_recalculate` makes the hidden div visible and updates its content (line 274), but does not remove or hide the original inline badge. After first recalculate, two premium/discount indicators appear side-by-side.

**Fix:** Remove inline `premiumHTML` from `buildHTML` initial render; populate `dcf-premium-{ticker}` on both initial render and recalculate.
